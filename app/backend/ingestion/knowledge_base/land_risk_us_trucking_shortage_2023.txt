Analysis: 2023 US Trucking & Rail Capacity Shortage

Event: A combination of a severe driver shortage (estimated 80,000 drivers) and a prolonged rail labor dispute.
Duration: Q2-Q4 2023.

Primary Impacts:

National "On-Time Delivery" (Land) metrics fell to 65%.

Freight costs (spot rates) for trucking surged by 45% in key lanes, especially LA-Chicago and LA-Dallas.

Rail container dwell times at West Coast ports (LA/LGB) doubled, as there was no capacity to move containers inland.

Major retailers (Walmart, Target) reported a 15% increase in inventory holding costs due to delayed inland transit.

Secondary Impacts:

Critical backlog at inland Distribution Centers (DCs) in Chicago, Dallas, and Atlanta.

"Last-mile" delivery networks were overwhelmed, delaying e-commerce shipments by an average of 3-5 days.

Companies that dual-sourced suppliers (e.g., using suppliers in both Mexico and Asia) had more resilience, as they could leverage cross-border trucking from Mexico.

Key Takeaway: The North American land network is heavily interdependent. A failure in one mode (rail) immediately overwhelms the other (trucking). The primary bottlenecks are always the major inland hubs (Chicago, Dallas, Atlanta) and the transfer points from seaports (LA/LGB).
